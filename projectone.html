Faux Museum Exhibition

A common graphic design assignment is to create a faux museum exhibition, based off a subject off the student's choosing. I chose: the sky. Naming it "Heaven Above Our Heads", as a subtle nod to how nearly every major religion came to the same conclusion: the sky is so beautiful, that must be where God(s) live.

This project taught me the importance of trial and error in design. Over the course of developing my ideas, I was diligent about making decent mockups at every phase of ideation, to ensure that I left no stone unturned in terms of design ideas. By front-loading my process with experimentation, sketching and troubleshooting, my overall project progression was way ahead of the class schedule, and once I completed my first deliverable; the rest were a breeze. Good design, as I learned throughout this project is 30% planning, 30% trying and failing and 40%; making it work irregardless.
